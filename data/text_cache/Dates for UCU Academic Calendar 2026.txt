10th December 2025  Dear Faculty, Staff and Students,  Re: Key Academic Dates for the 2026 Academic Year  I bring you warm greetings in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ.  Following approval of the Academic Schedule 2026, I wish to highlight key dates to guide academic, administrative and personal planning for the coming year. The detailed schedule remains the primary reference; the summary below is intended to draw attention to the main milestones.  1. Semester Dates – 2026 a) Easter (January) Semester 2026 Activity Date University opens for Easter Semester Monday, 5 January 2026 Formal beginning of semester & student reporting Wednesday, 7 January 2026 Lectures begin Monday, 12 January 2026 Examinations begin Tuesday, 7 April 2026 Semester ends Friday, 17 April 2026  b) Trinity (May) Semester 2026 Activity Date Beginning of semester & student reporting Wednesday, 22 April 2026 Lectures begin Monday, 27 April 2026 Examinations begin Monday, 20 July 2026 Semester ends Friday, 31 July 2026  c) Advent (September) Semester 2026 Activity Date Beginning of semester & student reporting Wednesday, 12 August 2026 Lectures begin Monday, 17 August 2026 Examinations begin Monday, 23 November 2026 Semester ends Friday, 4 December 2026

2. Graduation Ceremonies – 2026 Graduation Ceremony Date 27th Graduation Ceremony – Main Campus (Part 1) Friday, 3 July 2026 26th Graduation Ceremony – Main Campus (Part 2) Friday, 16 October 2026 26th Graduation Ceremony – Bishop Barham University College Friday, 30 October 2026 26th Graduation Ceremony – Mbale University College Friday, 13 November 2026  Detailed guidance on graduation clearance, submission of marks and publication of graduation lists will be issued by the Directorate of Academic Affairs and the respective colleges in due course.  3. Special Note: Physical Reporting for Easter Semester 2026  All students are reminded that the Easter Semester 2026 will commence on Monday, 5 January 2026, and that students are expected to report physically to their respective campuses and colleges in accordance with the dates indicated above. Teaching, learning and student support services for the Easter Semester will be organised on the basis of physical presence on campus from the beginning of the semester.  4. Special Note: Break for Presidential and Parliamentary Elections  In recognition of the national importance of the Presidential and Parliamentary Elections, the University will not teach from Wednesday, 14 January to Sunday, 18 January 2026. This short recess is intended to allow students and staff to travel to their respective polling stations and to participate fully in the electoral process and related civic responsibilities. Normal teaching and learning activities will resume on Monday, 19 January 2026.  I kindly request all Faculties, Schools, Departments and administrative units to align their internal plans, teaching timetables and assessment schedules with these dates. Students are similarly encouraged to plan their travel, accommodation, fieldwork and other engagements with these milestones in mind.  Thank you for your continued dedication to the mission of Uganda Christian University. I re-main grateful for your partnership as we prepare for a fruitful and orderly 2026 Academic Year.
